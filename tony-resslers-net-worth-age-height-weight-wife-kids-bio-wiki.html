    Career

    After graduating from the Columbia University, Ressler worked at the American bank and Wall Street firm, Drexel Burnham Lambert. In 1990, the bank got coerced into bankruptcy, and that led to the establishment of Apollo Global Management, an American private equity firm. Ressler and another co-founder, Leon Black, employed the heads of most of Drexel’s department.

    In 1997, he co-founded another asset management company called Ares Management, with his colleagues. The company sought to help its customers with asset management, real estate, stock trades, financial advice, investment consultancy, credit opportunities, etc. He stepped down and became the executive chairman in December 2017.

    In addition to this, he has also served as a minority stakeholder in the Major League’s Baseball team, Milwaukee Brewers, and as the principal owner of the Atlanta Hawks of the NBA, which he purchased for $800 million – $850 million.

    In the non-profit sector, he is a member of the Co-Chair Board of Trustees of the Los Angeles County Museum of Art and the Board of Directors of the Cedars-Sinai Medical Center. Apart from this, he is also the co-chairperson of Alliance for College-Ready Public Schools and one of the founders of the “Painted Turtle Camp,” an organization that takes ailing children on camping tours.

    He also supports the military through his contribution to The Greatest Generations Foundation.
